## 📝 Course Description  
This intensive **one-month TOEFL Preparation Course** is designed to equip students with the essential language skills, test strategies, and academic techniques required to achieve a competitive score on the TOEFL iBT.  

Throughout the program, students will engage in **interactive lectures, guided practice sessions, and simulated test activities** aimed at improving comprehension, fluency, and academic communication skills.  

Emphasis is placed on:  
- Developing **critical thinking and effective note-taking**  
- Building **clear argumentation and time management** under test conditions  

By the end of the course, participants will gain the **confidence and competence** needed to perform successfully in the TOEFL iBT and academic settings where English is the medium of instruction.  

---

## 📘 Assumed / Required Prior Knowledge  
No specific prerequisite is required.  
This course is ideal for **students and professionals** aiming to pursue **higher education or scholarships** in English-speaking environments. It serves as a **comprehensive preparation platform** for meeting English proficiency requirements in global universities and research programs.

## 🎯 Learning Outcomes  
By the end of this course, students will be able to:  
1. Identify the **structure, format, and scoring criteria** of the TOEFL iBT.  
2. Apply effective **reading, listening, speaking, and writing strategies**.  
3. Analyze **academic texts and lectures** for main ideas, details, and inferences.  
4. Demonstrate improved **vocabulary range, grammar, and coherence**.  
5. Construct **well-organized spoken and written responses** under time limits.  
6. Evaluate their own performance and implement **continuous improvement strategies**.

## 📅 Expected Course Schedule  

---

### 🗓 WEEK 1: Test Familiarization & Reading Skills  
**Objective:** Introduce the TOEFL structure and develop foundational reading comprehension strategies.  

#### **Day 1: Introduction to TOEFL iBT: Format, Scoring, and Diagnostic Assessment**
- Orientation and overview of TOEFL iBT format  
- Scoring criteria and time management  
- Diagnostic mini test covering all sections  

#### **Day 2: Reading Question Types — Facts, Vocabulary, Inference & Author’s Purpose**
🎯 *Goal:* Build understanding of details, vocabulary context, and implied meaning.  
Includes **Factual**, **Negative Factual**, **Vocabulary in Context**, **Reference**, **Inference**, and **Rhetorical Purpose** question types.  
- Mixed-type passage practice and feedback  
- Homework: Timed mini set (10 questions) + Academic word glossary (15 words)  

#### **Day 3: Reading Questions — Paraphrasing, Insertion, Summary & Organization**
🎯 *Goal:* Strengthen understanding of structure, coherence, and paraphrasing.  
Includes **Sentence Simplification**, **Insert Text**, **Prose Summary**, and **Organization / Table Completion**.  
- Practice and review session with strategy discussion  

#### **Day 4: Comprehensive Reading Review**
- Review all 10 question types with examples  
- Group discussion on correct and incorrect options  
- Reading strategies: Skimming, Scanning, Elimination  
- Vocabulary enhancement (15–20 academic words)  

#### **Day 5: Reading Section Simulation**
- Full Reading section (timed)  
- Review and error analysis  
- Vocabulary review and personalized feedback  

---

### 🗓 WEEK 2: Listening Comprehension & Note-Taking Skills  
**Objective:** Strengthen listening comprehension through structured practice and effective note-taking.  

#### **Day 1: Listening Questions — Main Ideas, Details, Function & Note-Taking Skills**
- Overview of Listening section  
- **Question Types:** Gist-Content, Detail, Function  
- Note-taking techniques: abbreviations, symbols, indentation  
- Practice: short clips + 1-minute note summaries  

#### **Day 2: Listening Questions — Attitude, Inference, Organization & Error Analysis**
- **Question Types:** Attitude, Inference, Organization  
- Emotional tone and inference recognition practice  
- Group discussion: avoiding traps through context-based verification  

#### **Day 3: Practice & Error Analysis**
🎯 *Goal:* Strengthen accuracy and listening focus.  
- Full lecture + conversation practice (TOEFL-style)  
- Answer review and strategy refinement  

#### **Day 4: Practice, Strategy & Score Boosting Techniques**
🎯 *Goal:* Learn test-smart methods for higher scores.  
- Scoring tips, recovery methods, elimination strategies  
- Active listening and predictive comprehension drills  

#### **Day 5: Complete Listening Test Practice**
- Timed listening section  
- Review and self-evaluation of note-taking effectiveness  

---

### 🗓 WEEK 3: Speaking Skills Development  
**Objective:** Enhance fluency, accuracy, and coherence in both independent and integrated speaking tasks.  

#### **Day 1: Speaking Overview**
- Speaking format and scoring overview  
- Introduction to Independent & Integrated tasks  
- Practice with Independent Task 1  

#### **Day 2: Independent Speaking Mastery**
- Organization and idea generation strategies  
- Practice on common question types  
- Pronunciation and intonation focus  

#### **Day 3: Integrated Speaking (Reading + Listening + Speaking)**
- Note-taking and summarization skills  
- Practice with campus-related tasks  

#### **Day 4: Integrated Speaking (Lecture + Question)**
- Linking ideas and transitions in speech  
- Delivery and pacing improvement  

#### **Day 5: Simulated Speaking Test**
- 4-task test simulation  
- Instructor and peer feedback  

---

### 🗓 WEEK 4: Writing Proficiency & Full Test Integration  
**Objective:** Develop academic writing skills and integrate all components through full-length practice.  

#### **Day 1: Writing Overview & Sample Analysis**
- Overview of Writing section  
- Scoring rubrics and evaluation  
- Sample essay analysis  

#### **Day 2: Integrated Writing Practice**
- Reading + Listening + Writing synthesis  
- Paraphrasing and connecting ideas  

#### **Day 3: Independent Writing Practice**
- Essay structure: introduction, body, conclusion  
- Cohesion, transitions, and lexical variety  

#### **Day 4: Grammar & Academic Vocabulary Workshop**
- Common grammatical and stylistic errors  
- Academic vocabulary enhancement  
- Quick quiz on Reading & Listening  

#### **Day 5: Full-Length TOEFL Mock Test**
- Complete TOEFL practice test (timed)  
- Individual feedback and strategy plan

## 💡 Study Tips  
Students are strongly encouraged to:  
- Actively participate in all **interactive sessions, practice tasks, and mock tests**.  
- Review class materials and maintain a **personal vocabulary journal**.  
- Simulate **real test conditions** at home to build time management and confidence.  
- Analyze mistakes from practice tests for steady progress.  
- Seek **continuous feedback** from instructors and peers for improvement.
